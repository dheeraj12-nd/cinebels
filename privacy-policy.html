1. Information Gathered by CPL through the Website

CPL gathers two basic types of information through the Website: (i) "Personal Information," information from which an individual can be identified, and (ii) "Aggregate Information," from which an individual cannot be identified.

  • Personal Information. You are not ordinarily required to register or provide Personal Information in order to access the Website. However, certain functionalities such as a membership program, through which purchasers of products may receive discounts on online purchases, participate in any contests, etc., may require registration. CPL collects Personal Information from you only when you voluntarily provide it to CPL - for example, in contacting CPL through the Website, answering surveys and polls, entering contests and similar promotions, signing up for email/SMS updates and announcements concerning the CPL's products and special promotions, and purchasing the CPL’s merchandise. Personal Information may include your contact information (such as your name, postal address, telephone number, and/or email address), as well as your payment information, such as credit card number and expiration date ("Payment Information"), which will be collected as part of the purchase transaction of any product or merchandise made available through the Website ("Transaction").
  • Aggregate Information. When you visit the Website, CPL and the third parties with whom the CPL has contracted to provide services may collect anonymous information from three sources: server log files, cookies, and pixel tags.
    • Your Internet Protocol (IP) address is an identifying number that is automatically assigned to your computer by your Internet Service Provider (ISP). This number is identified and logged automatically in the CPL’s server log files whenever you visit the Website, along with the time(s) of your visit(s) and the page(s) that you visited. IP addresses of all Visitors are used by the CPL to calculate the Website usage levels, to help diagnose problems with the Website's servers, and to administer the Website. CPL may also use IP addresses to communicate or to block access to Visitors who fail to comply with the Policy and/or the Terms and Conditions of Use of the Website. Collecting IP addresses is a standard practice followed on the Internet and is carried out automatically by many websites.
    • This website uses cookies. Cookies are data that a web server transfers to an individual's computer for record-keeping purposes. Cookies are an industry standard used by most websites and help facilitate users' on-going access to and use of a particular website. Cookies do not cause damage to the computer system or files, and only the website that transfers a particular cookie to the computer system can read, modify, or delete such cookie. If you do not want your information to be collected through the use of cookies, there is a simple procedure to be followed, in most browsers that allows the user that allows the user to delete existing cookies, automatically decline cookies, or be be given the choice of declining or accepting the transfer of particular cookies to the user's computer system. You should note, however, that declining cookies may make it difficult or impossible for you to access certain portions of the Website.
    • This website uses "Pixel Tags". The Website may use so-called "pixel tags," "web beacons," "clear GIFs" or similar means (collectively, "Pixel Tags") to compile aggregate statistics about Website usage and response rates. Pixel Tags enable CPL to maintain a count of the users who have visited certain pages of the Website. When used in HTML-formatted email messages, Pixel Tags can tell the sender whether and when the email was opened and read by the receiver of the e-mail.
